Introduction to Merge Sort

To understand Merge Sort, we will take the following example into consideration:

Imagine you have a big pile of cards, and each card has a number on it. Your goal is to arrange these cards from the smallest number to the biggest. But instead of trying to sort the whole pile at once, let's break it down into smaller, easier steps. This is what merge sort does!

Step 1: Break it Down

First, we split the big pile of cards into two smaller piles. Then, we keep splitting those piles until each pile has just one card. It's like tearing a big puzzle into tiny pieces.

Step 2: Sort the Small Pieces

Now that we have lots of tiny piles (each with just one card), we start putting them back together, but in the right order. We look at two small piles at a time, compare the cards, and put them in order, making a new pile.

Step 3: Merge the Piles

As we keep merging, the piles get bigger. We always compare the top card from each pile, pick the smaller one, and add it to the new pile. We keep doing this until all the cards are in one big pile again, but now they're all in order!

An Example:

Let's say you have 8 cards with the numbers 5, 3, 8, 6, 2, 7, 1, and 4.

  • Split: First, we split them into two groups: (5, 3, 8, 6) and (2, 7, 1, 4).
  • Split Again: Then, we split those groups: (5, 3) (8, 6) and (2, 7) (1, 4).
  • Split One More Time: Finally, we split them until we have single cards: (5) (3) (8) (6) (2) (7) (1) (4).

Now, we start merging:

  • Merge Small Piles: Compare and merge:
    • (5) and (3) become (3, 5)
    • (8) and (6) become (6, 8)
    • (2) and (7) become (2, 7)
    • (1) and (4) become (1, 4)
  • Merge Again: Compare and merge:
    • (3, 5) and (6, 8) become (3, 5, 6, 8)
    • (2, 7) and (1, 4) become (1, 2, 4, 7)
  • Final Merge: Compare and merge:
    • (3, 5, 6, 8) and (1, 2, 4, 7) become (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8)

And there you have it – all the cards are sorted!
